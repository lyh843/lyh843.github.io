Spatial proposal / Community / 2025

Together We Belong

A cross-cultural exchange space designed for international students in London. Rather than building from scratch, it imagines entering existing galleries or museums to create encounters, activities and belonging in a lighter, more movable way.

Credits

Designer
Shanyin Lei
Collaborators
Tiffany Pan, Aung Thant Kyaw, Ange Zhang
Methods
Research, questionnaire, workshop, spatial modelling, furniture design
PDF source
source/project_1/design production.pdf

The project responds to identity, cultural resonance and loneliness. Through a shared kitchen, cultural games, language exchange, exhibitions and writable walls, it gives people from different places a space to express themselves, move closer and rebuild confidence.

Inclusive

Low furniture, generous circulation, soft materials and tactile text respond to different bodily and sensory needs.

Flexible

The project imagines partnerships with museums, galleries, student unions and public institutions so the space can move between sites.

Social

Cooking, sports, games and exhibitions let cultural exchange begin through activity rather than explanation.

Final outcome

Process notes

From uncertainty to an enterable space

Research

Questionnaires and interviews showed that students wanted friendship, cultural learning and activities more than abstract cultural symbols.

Form

Early triangular structures and conveyor-belt ideas shifted into elliptical walls, transparent links and rounded passages to reduce distance.

Material

Soft, light and furry textiles were collected at Woolcrest Textiles LTD, then paired with questionnaire insights to choose calmer light colours.

Web

Later, models were brought into Spline and embedded in Cargo3 so each object could be clicked, rotated and viewed on mobile.
